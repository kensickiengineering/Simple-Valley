The Protein Industry’s Dirty Secret: Absorption Matters

Protein labels tell half the story. Bioavailability tells the truth.

Walk into any supplement shop and you’re surrounded by numbers. 20g of protein. 30g of protein.

We’re taught that protein is a math game. Hit your daily number and you’re covered.

But biology doesn’t work that way.

Emerging research into protein bioavailability—specifically a metric called DIAAS (Digestible Indispensable Amino Acid Score)—shows that not all proteins are created equal. In real terms, this means you can pay for 30 grams of protein, but your body may only absorb a fraction of it.

At Simple Valley, we don’t play the numbers game. We play the biology game. That’s why we built a specific protein matrix using Beef Protein Isolate, Hydrolyzed Collagen, and Beef Liver.

The Plant Protein Problem: Paying for What You Can’t Use

Plant-based proteins are everywhere. But from a digestive and biological standpoint, they face real limitations.

The absorption gap: Many plant proteins (such as pea or rice) exist within a fibrous matrix that resists digestion. They often score lower on the DIAAS scale, meaning a portion of their amino acids never make it into circulation.

The anti-nutrient tax: Plants contain compounds like phytic acid and lectins that evolved as defense mechanisms. Phytic acid can bind to minerals such as zinc, iron, and magnesium, reducing absorption. Lectins may irritate the gut lining in sensitive individuals.

The heavy metal concern: Because plants absorb minerals indiscriminately from soil, independent testing has shown that some plant protein powders can test higher for heavy metals like lead and cadmium compared to animal-based proteins.

Our verdict: We want nutrients, not defense mechanisms. Plants are excellent as side dishes, but they’re a compromised foundation for a high-performance protein bar.

The Whey Compromise: Fast Muscle, Slow Digestion

Whey protein became the industry standard for one reason: it works quickly. But speed comes with trade-offs.

The gut tax: Whey is a highly processed dairy byproduct. Even isolates can cause bloating, gas, or digestive discomfort in people who struggle with lactose or dairy proteins—a significant portion of the population.

The insulin spike: Whey triggers a rapid insulin response. This can be useful immediately post-workout, but outside that context, repeated insulin spikes may contribute to energy crashes and reduced metabolic flexibility.

Our verdict: Whey has its place. We just didn’t want a protein bar designed exclusively for bodybuilders in the gym. We wanted a bar you could eat during a hike or a workday without feeling like you swallowed a balloon.

The Whole-Animal Solution

So we asked a different question: What did humans evolve to eat?

The answer wasn’t powders or isolates alone. It was the whole animal. Muscle, connective tissue, and organs—each playing a specific biological role.

A. The Muscle: Beef Protein Isolate

Think of this as steak without the chewing. Beef Protein Isolate delivers a complete amino acid profile with a DIAAS score greater than 1.0 and is naturally rich in leucine—the amino acid that triggers muscle protein synthesis.

Because it’s dairy-free and lactose-free, it provides muscle repair without digestive stress.

B. The Balance: Hydrolyzed Collagen

Our ancestors didn’t just eat muscle meat. They consumed skin, cartilage, and broth—all rich in collagen.

Muscle meat is high in methionine, an amino acid that can be inflammatory when consumed without balance. Collagen is rich in glycine, which helps buffer methionine and supports connective tissue, joints, tendons, and skin.

By combining beef protein isolate with collagen, we restore the natural amino acid ratio of the whole animal.

C. The Spark: Beef Liver

Muscle provides fuel. Liver provides the spark.

Beef liver is among the most nutrient-dense foods on earth, delivering bioavailable vitamin A, B12, and copper—micronutrients your mitochondria need to actually convert food into energy.

Protein provides the fuel. Liver turns the engine on.

The Bottom Line

We didn’t throw ingredients into a blender to hit a macro target. We engineered a nose-to-tail nutritional system.

No bloating (dairy-free). No nutrient blockers (plant-free). No empty calories.

Just highly bioavailable protein and micronutrients designed for real routines, real days, and real output. This philosophy is built into every Simple Valley bar.
